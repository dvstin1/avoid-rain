# Modular Room Design Blueprint & Gameplay Mechanics

This layout guide establishes the geographical archetypes for our large-scale modular sub-maps, focusing on unique spatial flows and progression gates.

| Module Type | Spatial Flow / Layout Concept | Gameplay Gimmick & Hazard |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **The Cave** | Narrow, claustrophobic serpentine tunnels with frequent blind corners and dead ends. | **Line of Sight:** High concentration of bats hiding in shadows. Requires careful corner-checking. |
| **The Ruins** | Rigid, geometric matrix of interconnecting rooms, broken walls, and long linear corridors. | **Chokepoints:** Tight doorways make it easy to get cornered by heavy armored minibosses. |
| **The Village** | Clusters of small rectangular bounding boxes (buildings) with open pathways between them. | **Interior vs. Exterior:** Buildings offer safety from ranged threats, but trap you if swarmed. |
| **The Outpost** | High verticality. Long, horizontal ramparts connected by tight vertical ladder shafts or stairs. | **High Ground:** Ranged hazards target you from platforms above while you navigate the floor. |
| **The Forest** | Massive, open-floor layout with sparse, organic obstacle clusters (trees/rock pillars). | **Navigation Disorientation:** Lack of distinct walls makes it easy to lose your bearings or get flanked. |
| **The Pond** | Large central hazard zone (ink pools) forcing the player onto narrow exterior perimeter paths. | **Movement Restriction:** Stepping into the water applies a severe movement speed penalty. |
