# Lore Fragments: The Truncated Archive

This document archives the narrative snippets found throughout the Scriptorium. These fragments are manifestations of the Author's original intent, now shattered by The Bleed.

## 1. The Half-Cleft Manuscript
**Fragment ID:** `half_cleft_manuscript`
**Location:** Chapter 1 - Entrance Corridor
**Text:**
> "The quill was never meant to be a weapon. It was a tool of preservation. But when the ink began to boil, we had no choice but to sharpen the nibs. Half the world is now Cleft, separated by a margin that no longer holds its shape. If you are reading this, you are the last Editor. Please... finish the sentence we could not."

## 2. Technical Metadata
- **Standard Dimensions:** All Lore Lecterns ('L') are 1x1 tile entities.
- **Interaction:** [SPACE] key triggers a STANDARD dialogue modal.
- **Persistence:** Collected fragments are logged to the `discovered_lore` dictionary in the player's profile metrics.

## 3. Administrative Anomalies: The Special Editions (Item ID: `lore_fragment_02`)
*Context: A ledger fragment recovered from the Chronicler's vault.*

```text
"...The standard compilation follows a strict monthly cycle. These 'Monthly Reports' outline predictable geometry, stabilizing the rooms into recognizable corridors. 

However, inspectors must watch for the Cleft Indexing Glitch. Approximately once in every ten archival rolls, the ink turns thick and dark, forcing a 'Special Edition' matrix to compile. These editions do not match our standard files. They are dense with active threats, structural anomalies, and deep ink corruption. If you find yourself walking through a layout that feels distorted, know that you have stepped straight into an unredacted history..."
```

## 4. Geographical Classifications: The Distorted Regions

### The Bleached Forest (Item ID: `lore_fragment_forest`)
*"...The fibers of the timber do not grow from soil, but from layers of calcified parchment. The canopy behaves like a massive blotting sheet, soaking up the atmospheric moisture until the branches hang heavy, dripping dark residue onto the travelers below. To get lost here is to wander between lines of an unwritten natural history..."*

### The Sunken Pond (Item ID: `lore_fragment_pond`)
*"...A stagnant mirror of liquid carbon. It is not fed by streams, but by the runoff of abandoned drafts. Items dropped into its depths do not sink; they dissolve into raw syntax, their properties bleeding out until the water itself becomes corrosive to active memory..."*

### The Forgotten Scriptorium / Ruins (Item ID: `lore_fragment_ruins`)
*"...The structural pillars are composed of compressed ledger volumes, structural spines cracked under the weight of centuries. The halls loop non-linearly, folding back on themselves like an author rewriting the same tragic paragraph over and over again..."*

### The Deserted Outpost (Item ID: `lore_fragment_outpost`)
*"...A perimeter fortification built to guard the margins of the page. Its guard towers look out into an absolute void of unrendered white space. The soldiers who held it are long gone, but their ironclad routines remain etched into the stone..."*

### The Constellation Void (Item ID: `lore_fragment_void`)
*"...Patterns of absence sketched into the dark. These are not stars in a sky, but ink-stains on the reverse side of the page, visible only when the world is held up to the light of the Wellspring. They are instructions for the blankness, editorial marks that dictate how the Void will reclaim the narrative..."*

### The Market of Margins (Item ID: `lore_fragment_market`)
*"...A sliver of commerce found in the fold between chapters. Here, footnote-spirits trade in discarded annotations and misprinted glyphs. To buy here is to surrender a part of your own definition in exchange for a temporary correction to the world's cruel prose..."*
