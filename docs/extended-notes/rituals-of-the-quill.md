Rituals of the Quill

Throughout the Scriptorium, small rites are practiced to keep the Author's quill from slipping. They are simple, quiet acts: a
three-fold tie of the page, a whispered punctuation, a small offering of unused glyphs laid at the Wellspring's rim. The rituals
are folk-practices more than magic, but they have real effects on the manuscript's temperament.

Common rituals include:

- The Quiet Fold: Creasing a page exactly at the margins slows the onset of The Bleed in the next room for the first minute.
- The Ink-Offering: Sacrificing a minor Torn Page to the Wellspring refreshes a single Respite and can remove one minor
  corruption token from the player's frame.
- The Redaction Chant: A short chorus sung near the Market of Margins that temporarily convinces sellers to accept different
  forms of payment (health instead of pages, for example).

In lore, rituals are the repository of communal memory. They are the small human resistances against the Author's indifference.
Players who collect ritual notes or repeat them enough times sometimes cause emergent changes: a vendor will add an extra scrap,
a Respite's glyph will glow a different color, or the Chronicler will hum a new, nearly-lost verse.
