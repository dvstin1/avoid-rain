. The Lore: "The Fading Draft"

To make this beautiful visual transition fit perfectly into your manuscript universe, we can define this monochrome state as the "Bleaching Process."

    The Lore: When your health hits zero, the Author's text-erasure strike doesn't just kill you—it actively strips the meaning from your character's page. Color in the Libram represents active, living intent. Going monochrome means the ink is being violently drained of its purpose.

    The Freeze: The 5-second freeze is the terrifying moment where your character's draft becomes completely static. The world around you freezes, hardens into raw, cold print, and loses its vitality.

    The Fade: The final fade to black is the literal closing of the book. The chapter is being abandoned, crumpled up, and thrown away by the Author, leaving nothing but a dark, empty canvas before the Scriptorium reopens a fresh, blank page for your next run.
