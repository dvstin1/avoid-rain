Market of Margins

The Market of Margins sits in the thin space between two pages — a sliver of commerce where discarded notes, forgotten annotations
and misprinted glyphs are traded like coin. It appears only to those who linger too long at the Scriptorium's edge and listen
for the whisper of loose leaves.

Vendors are footnote-spirits, half-ink and half-membrance. They trade services as often as wares: a sewn correction for a scarred
memory, an eraser-stamp that makes a single sentence unreadable, a scrap that will confuse a minor Censor for a breath.

Mechanically, a Market purchase is a temporary modification to the draft: a margin-note you paste onto the world. It may reroute
a single enemy's patrol, soften a spike of rainfall for one room, or preserve a Respite's glow for an extra minute. The price is
usually literal: something marginal must be surrendered — a stat tweak, one Torn Page, or a memorized glyph.

In lore, the Margins are also a moral mirror. Buyers who constantly purchase erasures risk becoming footnote-ghosts, people who
exist only in parenthetical aside. Long-term use is rumored to thin the Binder's patience; stitched pages that bear margin-marks
can sometimes unbind more easily, becoming candidates for future corruption.
