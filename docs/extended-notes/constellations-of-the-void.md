Constellations of the Void

When the Author pauses and stares into the margins, they sometimes doodle. Those doodles, inked in absence, form the
Constellations of the Void — night-maps visible only from the Wellspring's surface. Each pattern is an instruction, a tiny
editorial command that guides how blankness will try to reclaim a paragraph.

Players who learn to read these constellations gain informal foresight: the pattern over a ramp or corridor may predict where the
Bleed will pool at night, or which rooms will spawn a minor Censor. Finding a star-chart and tracing it in the hub grants a brief
clarity buff that reduces rain intensity for a single run.

Lore-wise, the constellations are the Author's sleep-addresses. They are small marks of where the mind went wandering when the
quill slipped. Scholars debate whether they are accidental or deliberate—an encoded apology disguised as a sky of empty shapes.

A hidden consequence: drawing a new constellation in the Market of Margins or at a Respite is said to lure a dream-beast into the
manuscript. Some players treat the charts as tools; others as temptations to summon something that may never wish to leave.
