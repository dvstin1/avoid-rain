    The Run Metaphor: When a player steps into the game world, they are literally being projected into the ink-and-parchment reality written inside the book. Because the book is corrupted, the ink shifts every time it is closed and reopened, seamlessly explaining your procedural map generation.

    The Goal: You are fighting your way to the final chapter to confront the entity rewriting reality, trying to force a "The End" that finally breaks the loop.

You mentioned that enemies drop unreadable torn pages used to level up. Here is a compelling narrative reason for why they can't be read, and how they make you stronger:

    The Lore: The monsters roaming the halls are composed of stolen text—malicious, rewritten prose. When you destroy them, their formatting shatters, leaving behind Unbound Margin Pages.

    The Level-Up Mechanic: The silent NPC in your hub area is The Binder. While you cannot read the pages, the Binder can programmatically stitch these fragments into your skin or armor. You aren't learning new skills through study; you are literally hacking your character's stat values by pasting foreign text onto your soul.

4. The Co-op Twist: Shared Reading

Your plan for local network co-op fits beautifully into this library aesthetic without needing a complex explanation.

    The Lore: When another player on your Wi-Fi network boots up the game, they are simply opening a second copy of the exact same book.

    The Co-op Loop: By joining a world, they are choosing to "Read Along." Two minds are focusing on the same paragraph at the same time, manifesting both players inside the exact same draft to tackle the hostile narrative together.

On its surface, "Avoid Rain" sounds like a simple, literal arcade objective—which is great because it's punchy and memorable. But with your new book-binding lore, that title gains a dark, clever double meaning that ties the gameplay mechanics directly to the plot.

Here are three distinct ways "Avoid Rain" perfectly anchors your game's universe:
1. The Literal & The Metaphorical Warning

Within the world of the Libram, the characters you meet in the Scriptorium talk about the rain with absolute dread. To them, it isn't a natural weather phenomenon; it is a weaponized structural collapse.

    The Lore: "Avoid Rain" is the absolute primary rule of survival passed down by previous "readers" who became trapped in the archive. To touch the rain is to let the corrupt ink dissolve your very definition, washing away your stats and code strings.
