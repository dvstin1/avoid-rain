Binder Scripts

The Binder is more than an NPC: it is a procedural artisan that writes literal glue between you and the torn pages. Its workshop
is full of needles, ruled tape, and a small loom that hums like a metronome. Binder Scripts are patterns the Binder can weave
into you when given the right fragments.

Each Script is an operational metaphor: a "Hemming Script" stitches a small hit-point buffer into the edges of your frame; an
"Indexing Script" rewrites one enemy type to drop a specific marginal scrap more often. Scripts are not spells—they are tiny
compilers running in the margins, altering how the draft allocates resources.

Because they are mechanical, Scripts can be combined but are subject to syntax conflicts. Two Scripts that both touch the same
stat will fight; the stronger or more complete script wins. That is why you sometimes find half-woven patterns in the Market of
Margins or in the backs of Respite pedestals.

In lore, the Binder's true desire is to perfect the stitch. It keeps a secret ledger of failed Scripts, each one a possible path
to a different ending. Some believe that completing a particular sequence of Binder Scripts will make the Libram hold, ending the
cycle of ink-bleed.
