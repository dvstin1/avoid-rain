Ink and the Menagerie

The creatures of the manuscript are not born so much as over-written. Ink flows, pools, and then congeals around a concept until a
creature is formed. The Menagerie is the collective name for these beings: marginal motes, chapter-sprites, Censors, and the rare
Typographes — animals that look like punctuation brought to life.

Small entry-level foes are Flutters and Smears: living scrawls that flutter away when you approach. Mid-tier threats are Bindlings -
fragments of a failed footnote that gained hunger. Each played species shows its origin in presence: runic streaks across their
flanks, misaligned punctuation growing like fur.

Collecting a Menagerie index (a miniature bestiary) is possible at the Wellspring. Each sighting writes a new line into the hub's
mirror; perfecting an index grants mechanical benefits like reduced spawn variety or increased drop chance for certain Torn Pages.

The lore angle: some scholars argue the Menagerie is not wholly violent; they are attempts by the manuscript to self-edit. Killing
one is like removing a typo; taming or cataloging it is like preserving a useful variation.
